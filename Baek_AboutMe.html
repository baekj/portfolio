Hello, my name is Joanne Baek. I am currently a high school senior attending Dublin High School. While peanut butter and jelly is a sweet combination, I find biology and engineering to be even better! Thus, I plan on studying chemical & biomolecular engineering at Johns Hopkins Univeristy this coming fall. My participation in the Dublin Engineering and Design Academy has played an integral role in shaping my career goals. Right now I am enrolled in the Digital Electronics (DE) course, where I learn about circuits and eventually build my own computer. In the past, I have taken the Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE) course as well as the Principles of Engineering (POE) course through the academy.

Some major accomplishments of my high school career are receiving both the Academic Block D and Honor Pin, co-founding two clubs, and being a California Scholarship Federation Lifetime Member. The majority of my classes are rigorous and geared towards my future career. Due to their level of difficulty, I have often pushed myself to improve my work ethic and mindset. For example, POE taught me to be comfortable working in a team and presenting in front of the class, both of which I was hesistant to do before.

Everything that I do, whether I am volunteering in Nepal or studying in my room, has made me who I am today: tenacious, dedicated, and meticulous. As I mature and prepare for college, I am slowly learning to balance several aspects of my life at once while putting my best efforts into all of them. Please feel free to explore the rest of my website to learn all about these different facets.
