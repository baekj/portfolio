Slow Fade LED Circuit - PCB

2017, Digital Electronics (DE)

The Slow Fade LED Circuit was an individual project in which the objective was to ultimately create my own printed circuit board (PCB). In order to do this, I had to define a circuit, test it on a breadboard, simulate it using the program NI Multisim, fabricate it using the program Fritzing, etch it, and solder the components on myself. Through this process, I was able to learn about the creation of PCB's as well as how to develop and refine a circuit. The circuit's design was dictated by certain criteria, which included an electronic documentation. A video of the PCB working can be accessed here.

Image Artist - Python

2017, Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE)

The Image Artist project was done by me and my partner on Cloud 9. The objective of the project was to manipulate multiple images in the same way using an algorithm programmed in Python. My team had to manipulate the images appropiately to cater to our client, which we decided would be an animal shelter called Furry Friends Shelter. Throughout the duration of the project, we documented our progress and satisfied a set of criteria. I contributed by coding half of the algorithm and updating the documentation. Space Invaders - MIT App Inventor

2016, Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE)

Space Invaders is an Android app created on MIT App Inventor by me and my partner. The objective of the project was to follow a basic tutorial and make our own stylistic changes to it based on a set of criteria. My partner and I decided to make changes to alter the difficulty and complexity of the game in a time frame of two weeks. I contributed by coding and documenting the application's development. The app can be downloaded (link in documentation) and tested on any Android device.

Dog Mario - Scratch

2016, Computer Science/Software Engineering (CSE)

Dog Mario is a simple platform game created through Scratch, a basic programming language. The objective of the project was to design, code, and document a game fulfilling a certain criteria. Over a period of two weeks my partner and I coded and tested in small increments to ensure the functionality of the game. We developed a beta and final version of the game, both of which underwent peer review. I contributed by coding about half of the game as well as keeping a thorough documentation of the project's development.

unSalt Me - Dublin Entrepreneur Project

2016, Principles of Engineering (POE)

As part of a four-member team I created unSalt Me, a portable desalination bottle, to be a solution to water scarcity for the Dublin Entrepreneur Project. We had a time frame of about 2 weeks to brainstorm, create a solution, and prepare a 3 minute presentation as well as an electronic documentation. My contributions included research, documentation, and construction of the physical model. My research dealt with the need for our product, such as statistics on water scarcity. Afterwards, I compiled this information onto the electronic documentation and the presentation, focusing mainly on customization.

Solar Hydrogen Project

2015, Principles of Engineering (POE)

The Solar Hydrogen Vehicle was a vehicle constructed by me and three other team members for a project in POE. The objective of the project was to design, create, and test a prototype of a vehicle that will be powered by two interchangeable energy sources that will be interchangeable: solar panels and hydrogen fuel cells. As the project manager, I kept the team on task, managed work time, and ensured we met all requirements. As a main documenter, I spent an extensive amount of time documenting the our process. Overall, I gained leadership skills, time management skills, and technical writing skills.
